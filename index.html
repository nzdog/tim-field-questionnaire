Field Questionnaire

for Tim – Expanded

Purpose

To help you sense the field you're operating in—the invisible architecture shaping your pace, decisions, and energy.

How to Use

Take these questions slowly. You don't need perfect answers. Just notice what feels true, even in fragments.

Question 1

What feels "normal" in your work right now?

What feels "normal" often reveals the invisible rules of your field. It shows whether your baseline is regenerative or extractive.

Examples:
"It feels normal to be behind and catching up."
"It feels normal to have long stretches of creative flow, then rest."
"It feels normal to push out content quickly even if it isn't perfect."
Question 2

What does your field reward you for?

Fields shape behavior by rewarding certain actions. If you see what gets rewarded, you see the values driving your work.

Examples:
"I get attention when I post fast, not when I go deep."
"Clients praise me for being reliable and thoughtful."
"Investors light up when I talk about scale, not sustainability."
Question 3

How does your field hold you when things get hard?

The way challenges are treated shows if your field is extractive (punishing) or regenerative (composting into learning).

Examples:
"When a video doesn't land, I feel like the whole project is failing."
"When I miss a week, I can reflect and improve next time."
"If sales dip, I get pressure from outside voices to work harder."
Question 4

What's the quality of your decisions in this field?

Decision quality is a signal of field health. Coherent fields create clarity; extractive fields create reactivity.

Examples:
"I often make decisions late at night under stress."
"I notice the best decisions come when I step back for a day."
"I'm pushed to decide quickly, even when I don't have enough signal."
Question 5

What is the cost of staying in this field?

Every field asks for something—energy, time, relationships. Seeing the cost helps you weigh if the field is worth it.

Examples:
"I'm energized but I neglect rest."
"I get traction but it strains my partner relationship."
"I grow fast but feel disconnected from the original purpose."
Question 6

What kind of field would you want to be in?

Clarity on the desired field helps you see the gap between where you are and where you could be. It also names the qualities you most need for resilience.

Examples:
"I'd love a field where deep work is valued more than speed."
"I want a field that supports creative play, not just output."
"I'd choose a field where trust builds momentum instead of pressure."

Closing Reflection

You don't need to label your field as "good" or "bad." Most fields are mixed. The invitation is simply to see the architecture you're in—and decide whether it's the one you want shaping your future.
